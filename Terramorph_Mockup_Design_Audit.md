# Terramorph Mockup Design Audit — Professional Polish + Engagement Upgrade Plan

Audit date: 2026-06-05  
Audited artifact: `/Users/Jarvis/Desktop/Terramorph-Mockup/`  
Scope: the clickable mockup I created, not the old live site.  
Goal: identify what still needs to improve before this feels like a truly premium, high-converting, professional landscaping website.

## Executive Verdict

The mockup is directionally strong: it fixes the biggest strategic issue by repositioning Terramorph around higher-value landscaping, patios, drainage, and outdoor living instead of a generic lawn-care brochure. It has the right conversion skeleton: strong H1, service-specific pages, sticky CTAs, short quote funnel, trust strip, gallery, and local SEO architecture.

But visually and professionally, it is still a **strong v1 prototype**, not yet a $25k+ final web concept. The biggest opportunity is to replace “good landing page structure” with a more premium, emotionally engaging outdoor-living brand experience.

1. **Clearer positioning**

## Biggest Weaknesses Holding It Back

### 1. The design still feels more like a clean prototype than a luxury outdoor-living brand

The current look is clean and usable, but not yet emotionally premium. A high-end landscaping site should make the homeowner feel: “I want my backyard to look like that.”

Current issue:
- Sections are functional but predictable.
- The service cards and trust cards feel template-like.
- The brand lacks a distinct visual signature beyond green/earth tones.

Improve with:
- More cinematic hero composition.
- Better photography hierarchy.
- Editorial-style project storytelling.
- Richer textures: stone, soil, blueprint lines, subtle topographic lines, paver grid patterns.
- More custom section shapes instead of repeated cards.

Priority: Critical  
Impact: Very high  
Difficulty: Medium

### 2. Hero image does not sell the highest-value outcome hard enough

The hero image is decent, but it does not scream “premium patio / outdoor living transformation.” For a $25k+ concept, the above-the-fold image should be the strongest possible backyard/patio/outdoor-living image.

Current issue:
- Hero is landscape/yard oriented, not aspirational enough.
- It does not instantly show a dream outdoor space.
- The emotional value is weaker than the headline.

Improve with:
- Use a premium patio/outdoor living hero: seating area, fire pit, lighting, stonework, finished landscaping.
- Add a small “Designed / Built / Maintained locally” badge.
- Use an image stack or photo slider directly in the hero.
- Add a small “Tap to compare photo” interaction on mobile.

Priority: Critical  
Impact: Very high  
Difficulty: Low to medium

### 3. Trust logos are present but visually underpowered

Google Verified and BBB are now included, but they are small inside trust cards. They read as supporting details rather than major credibility anchors.

Current issue:
- Logo treatment is too small.
- The BBB/Google cards are visually equal to generic trust text.
- There are no review snippets or star proof near the logos.

Improve with:
- Create a dedicated “Trusted by homeowners across Perrysburg & Toledo” band.
- Use larger Google Verified and BBB logos on white cards.
- Add 2–3 real review snippets if available.
- Add “Verified on Google” and “Better Business Bureau profile” context.
- Add CTA under trust: “Read reviews” / “Get a quote from a verified local crew.”

Priority: High  
Impact: High  
Difficulty: Low

### 4. The homepage lacks a strong “problem → transformation” story

The current homepage jumps from hero to service cards to proof. That is clear, but not deeply persuasive.

Current issue:
- It tells people what Terramorph does, but does not fully dramatize why they should act.
- Homeowners are motivated by frustration: muddy yard, boring builder-grade landscaping, cracked patio, poor drainage, no place to host.

Improve with a new section:

“Your yard should not feel like wasted space.”

Pain cards:
- Standing water after every storm
- Builder-grade front beds with no curb appeal
- No patio space for hosting
- Sloped yard or drainage issues
- Landscaping that looks good for one season then fails

Then transformation promise:
- Designed for Northwest Ohio weather
- Built around drainage and longevity
- Finished to improve curb appeal and outdoor living

Priority: High  
Impact: High  
Difficulty: Medium

### 5. Service cards need more premium detail and price/value framing

The current three service cards are clear, but too generic. High-ticket service pages should signal quality, process, and outcomes.

Current issue:
- Cards mostly say service category + short description.
- They do not show enough visual or practical differentiation.

Improve service cards with:
- “Best for” labels.
- Starting scope examples.
- Outcome-focused bullets.
- Mini trust proof.
- Service-specific CTA.

Example:

Paver Patios & Hardscapes  
Best for: outdoor hosting, fire pits, walkways, backyard upgrades  
Includes: base prep, drainage planning, paver installation, finishing edges  
CTA: “Plan My Patio”

Priority: High  
Impact: High  
Difficulty: Low

### 6. Quote funnel is functional but not persuasive enough

The form is the right length, but the area around it should do more selling.

Current issue:
- Form copy explains mechanics instead of reassuring the homeowner.
- No “what happens next” beside the form on the homepage form section.
- No privacy/reassurance line.

Improve with:

- Add three next-step cards next to/below form: Request → Review → Book estimate.
- Add inline validation and better phone keyboard input.

Priority: High  
Impact: High  
Difficulty: Low

### 8. Service pages need deeper sales copy and FAQs

The service pages exist and have structure, but they are still thin for SEO and conversion.

Current issue:
- They have H1, proof bullets, process, and form — good for a mockup.
- They need more real substance to become production-grade landing pages.

Improve each service page with:

Priority: High

Difficulty: Medium

### 9. Accessibility is the biggest technical weakness

Lighthouse accessibility is currently 63. That is not acceptable for a production-quality site.

Observed Lighthouse issues:
- Color contrast failures.
- Some images missing alt attributes.
- Form labels not properly associated with inputs/selects.
- Select elements missing accessible names.
- No main landmark.
- Image width/height not explicitly set.

Improve with:
- Wrap main content in `<main>`.
- Give every form label a `for` attribute and every input/select an `id`.
- Add descriptive alt text to service/gallery images.
- Improve contrast on muted green text and trust-card text.
- Add width/height attributes or CSS aspect-ratio to major images.
- Add visible focus states.
- Make file input styling clearer.

Priority: Critical  
Impact: High for professionalism, SEO hygiene, UX  
Difficulty: Low to medium

The mockup has service pages, but Google Ads and Meta Ads would benefit from purpose-built landing-page variants.

Each should have:

- One CTA

Priority: Medium-high

## Bottom Line

The current mockup is a strong strategic foundation, but it should not be treated as final design yet. The next version should focus less on “website structure” and more on **premium emotional selling**:

- bigger transformation imagery
- stronger homeowner pain/solution story
- more real proof
- more polished trust logos/reviews
- stronger service-specific sales copy
- better accessibility
- richer brand texture

If I were improving it next, I would start with the homepage and create a v2 that feels less like a prototype and more like a polished outdoor-living brand: cinematic hero, upgraded proof band, better service modules, featured project photo, homeowner pain section, and a more persuasive quote funnel.
